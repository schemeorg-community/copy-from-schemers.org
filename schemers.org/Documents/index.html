Introductory Texts

These are textbooks dedicated to the introductory programming curriculum and/or beginning Schemers.

  • Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (2/e), Harold Abelson, Gerald Jay Sussman with Julie Sussman, MIT Press, 1996
  • La programmation: une approche fonctionnelle et récursive avec Scheme, Laurent Arditi and Stéphane Ducasse, Eyrolles, 1996
  • Initiation à la programmation avec Scheme, Laurent Bloch, Editions Technip, 2001
  • Programmation récursive (en Scheme), Anne Brygoo, Titou Durand, Maryse Pelletier, Christian Queinnec and Michèle Soria, Dunod, 2004
  • Programmation récursive -- annales corrigées novembre 2001-septembre 2005, Anne Brygoo, Titou Durand, Maryse Pelletier, Christian Queinnec and Michèle Soria, Paracamplus, 2006
  • Programmer avec Scheme: De la pratique à la théorie, Jacques Chazarain, International Thomson Publishing, 1996
  • Passeport pour Scheme, Robert Cori and Pierre Casteran, unpublished manuscript
  • The Scheme Programming Language (3/e), R. Kent Dybvig, MIT Press, 2003
  • How to Design Programs, Matthias Felleisen, Robert Bruce Findler, Matthew Flatt and Shriram Krishnamurthi, MIT Press, 2001 (available in full on-line, date is for print version)
  • The Schemers Guide (2/e), Iain Ferguson with Edward Martin and Burt Kaufman, Schemers Inc., 1995
  • The Little Schemer (4/e), Daniel P. Friedman and Matthias Felleisen, MIT Press, 1995
  • The Seasoned Schemer, Daniel P. Friedman and Matthias Felleisen, MIT Press, 1995
  • Exploring Computer Science with Scheme, Oliver Grillmeyer, Springer-Verlag, 1998
  • Concrete Abstractions: An Introduction to Computer Science Using Scheme, Max Hailperin, Barbara Kaiser and Karl Knight, PWS Publishing, 1998
  • Sketchy Scheme (4.5th ed), Nils M. Holm, self-published (the 4.0 edition, called Sketchy LISP but using Scheme, is available for free)
  • Simply Scheme, Brian Harvey and Matthew Wright, MIT Press
  • Programmation Fonctionnelle en Scheme, Jean-Michel Hufflen, Masson, Paris, 1996
  • Die Macht der Abstraktion, Herbert Klaeren und Michael Sperber, Teubner, 2007
  • Vom Problem zum Programm: Architektur und Bedeutung von Computerprogrammen (3/e), Herbert Klaeren und Michael Sperber, Teubner, 2001
  • The Schematics of Computation, Vincent Manis and James Little, Prentice-Hall, 1995
  • Recueil de Petits Problèmes en Scheme, Luc Moreau, Christian Queinnec, Daniel Ribbens and Manuel Serrano, Springer, 1999
  • Programming and Meta Programming in Scheme (with errata), Jon Pearce, Springer, 1998
  • Débuter la programmation avec Scheme, Jean-Christophe Routier and Eric Wegrzynowski, International Thompson Publishing France, 1997
  • Premiers cours de programmation avec Scheme, Jean-Paul Roy, Ellipses, 2010
  • Teach Yourself Scheme in Fixnum Days, Dorai Sitaram, Web document
  • Scheme and the Art of Programming, George Springer and Daniel P. Friedman, MIT Press/McGraw Hill, 1990

Advanced Texts

These are texts on advanced material that either use Scheme as the object of study or employ Scheme to illustrate key ideas.

  • The Reasoned Schemer, Daniel P. Friedman, William E. Byrd, and Oleg Kiselyov, MIT Press, 2005
  • Essentials of Programming Languages (2/e), Daniel P. Friedman, Mitchell Wand and Christopher T. Haynes, MIT Press, 2001
  • Programming Languages: Application and Interpretation, Shriram Krishnamurthi, 2003
  • Principles of Programming Languages, Shriram Krishnamurthi and Matthias Felleisen, Rice University Department of Computer Science Technical Report CS TR 97-292 — this has now been superceded by the previous entry
  • Principes d'implantation de Scheme et Lisp, Christian Queinnec, Paracamplus, 2007: formerly Les Langages Lisp, Christian Queinnec, InterEditions, 1994 (or Lisp in Small Pieces, Cambridge University Press)
  • The Structure and Interpretation of Classical Mechanics, Gerald Jay Sussman and Jack Wisdom, MIT Press, 2001
  • An Introduction to Scheme and its Implementation, Paul Wilson (book-in-progress)
